# Automation Rules (Miner Controller DSL)

The absolute core of the Solar Miner App is the **Miner Controller DSL** (Domain Specific Language). This powerful engine allows you to build highly granular automation rules—from simple "On/Off" switches to complex, time-based average calculations across a cluster of multiple ASICs.

Every rule in the system consists of two main components:
1. **The Condition (IF):** When should something happen?
2. **The Action (THEN):** What exactly should the miners do?

---

## 1. Variables (What You Measure)

Variables represent the real-time data fetched from your PV inverter, smart meter, or Home Assistant setup.

* **`PV_PRODUCTION`:** The current solar power generated by your panels (in kW).
* **`LOADS_POWER`:** Your current house consumption / base load (in kW).
* **`PV_SURPLUS`:** The calculated excess solar power (in kW). Internally calculated as: `PV_PRODUCTION - LOADS_POWER`.
* **`PV_EXPORT`:** The actual power exported to the public grid (in kW).
* **`PV_IMPORT`:** The power currently bought from the grid (in kW).
* **`BATTERY_SOC`:** The State of Charge of your home battery in percentage (0% to 100%).

---

## 2. Time Aggregations (Smoothing Data)

Clouds pass by. Spikes in house consumption happen (e.g., turning on a kettle). To prevent your ASICs from rapidly turning on and off, the DSL allows you to analyze historical data over a specific timeframe. 

Instead of looking at a single second, you can apply **Aggregations** to a timeframe (`MINUTES`, `HOURS`, `DAYS`):

* **`LAST`:** Takes the most recent, real-time value.
* **`MEAN`:** Calculates the average value over the timeframe (Perfect for smoothing out passing clouds).
* **`MIN` / `MAX`:** Finds the absolute lowest or highest peak in the chosen timeframe.
* **`MEDIAN`:** Finds the middle value, making your rule highly resistant to extreme data outliers.
* **`MODE`:** The most frequently occurring value during the timeframe.
* **`INTEGRAL`:** Calculates the area under the curve (useful for converting kW over time into actual kWh energy values).
* **`SUM` / `COUNT`:** The total sum or amount of data points collected.
* **`STANDARD_DEVIATION` / `VARIANCE`:** Advanced statistical tools to measure data volatility.

---

## 3. Comparators & Logical Operators

To evaluate your variables and aggregations, you use standard comparators and logical groupings.

**Comparators:**
* **`EQUAL (=)`:** The value must match exactly.
* **`LESS (<)` / `LESS_OR_EQUAL (<=)`:** The value is smaller, or smaller/equal.
* **`GREATER (>)` / `GREATER_OR_EQUAL (>=)`:** The value is larger, or larger/equal.

**Logical Grouping (Groups):**
You can nest multiple conditions into groups to handle complex scenarios.
* **`AND`:** *All* conditions inside this group must be true.
* **`OR`:** *At least one* condition inside this group must be true.
* **`NOT`:** Inverts the result of the condition.

---

## 4. Controller Actions (What the Miner Does)

If a rule's condition evaluates to `TRUE`, the engine triggers an action. 
*(Note: Power values must be entered as absolute Watt numbers).*

* **`SET_POWER_TARGET` (`power`):** Sets the miner's power limit to a fixed value (e.g., "1500" for 1500 Watts). If the target is > 0W, the miner automatically resumes mining.
* **`INCREMENT_POWER_TARGET` (`power_increment`):** Increases the current power limit by a specific Watt amount.
* **`DECREMENT_POWER_TARGET` (`power_decrement`):** Decreases the current power limit by a specific Watt amount. If the limit hits 0W, the miner pauses.
* **`PAUSE` (`pause`):** Completely stops the hashing process and drops power consumption to idle.
* **`RESUME` (`resume`):** Restarts the hashing process and instantly sets the power target to the miner's maximum hardware limit.

---

## 5. Cluster Targets & Strategies

If you are running more than one ASIC, you need to tell the system how to distribute the commands.

**Action Target Type:**
* **`SINGLE_MINER`:** Legacy compatibility mode for one machine.
* **`ALL_MINERS`:** The action (e.g., `PAUSE`) is broadcasted to all miners simultaneously.
* **`CLUSTER_DYNAMIC`:** The app intelligently decides which miner receives the command based on your chosen strategy.

**Miner Distribution Strategy (Requires `CLUSTER_DYNAMIC`):**
* **`SEQUENTIAL`:** Miners are prioritized one by one. (e.g., Miner 1 is ramped up to 100% capacity before Miner 2 receives any power increases).
* **`EQUAL_DISTRIBUTION`:** The requested power adjustment is divided equally among all active miners in the cluster.

## 💡 Practical Example Scenario

Let's say you want to build a safe "Start Mining" rule that respects your home battery and ignores short sun spikes.

**The Goal:** Start the miner at 2000 Watts ONLY IF the home battery is nearly full (> 90%) AND the average solar surplus over the last 10 minutes has been consistently higher than 2.5 kW.

**How it looks in the App:**
* **Rule Name:** `Start_Miner_Safe`
* **Action:** `power` -> Value: `2000` | Target: `ALL_MINERS`
* **Condition Group:** `AND`
  * **Predicate 1:** `LAST` from `BATTERY_SOC` `>=` `90` (since 0 Seconds)
  * **Predicate 2:** `MEAN` from `PV_SURPLUS` `>=` `2.5` (since 10 Minutes)

With this setup, passing clouds won't shut off your miner, and short sun rays won't drain your battery!
